# 2026-07-20: m7/6 policy decision

**Goal.** Record the owner decision closing the musical review of the m7/6
inversion family (`../m7-sixth-family-notes.md`, log entry 2026-07-19-04).

**Setup.** The review presented both traditions with sources: lead-sheet
practice names the bass-on-third voicing as the added-sixth chord (Brandt &
Roemer, Levine, Berklee subdominant-minor function), while common-practice
analysis reads the seventh chord in inversion (Aldwell & Schachter, tertian
root-finding), with the 300-year-old consensus (Rameau's double emploi) that
context decides. Cross-tab of the 1,344 DCML dev misses by expected quality,
degree, mode, and applied status sized the options.

**What happened.** The owner adopted the context-conditional design (option C of
the notes) and resolved the contested middle toward the lead-sheet-audience
readings over classical analysis. Concretely, the lever 0 rule scope:

- Prefer the seventh-in-inversion reading, under the prevailing key, only in the
  genre-agreed cells: a minor7 whose root sits on degree ii (both modes; 239 dev
  misses), a half-diminished7 whose root sits on degree ii in minor (the jazz
  minor ii-V chord; 344), and the half-diminished7 leading-tone family (m6-on-II
  is not an idiom in either culture; 130 plus tail). Coverage: 721 of 1,344
  misses (54%).
- Keep the sixth-chord reading everywhere else, deliberately including: the
  borrowed major-key iiø65, which stays Fm6 (IVm6, the Berklee subdominant-minor
  slot; the owner's explicit call, 144 dev misses stay as audience-correct
  disagreements with the analysts); the vi65-vs-I6 cases, which stay I6 (tonic
  sixth); and the smaller VI/III-rooted tail.
- Applied figures (259) are out of scope for a local-key rule by design.
- Flagged for a possible future review of the same kind: minor-key iv7 named as
  VI6 (52 cases), where jazz idiom arguably also favors the m7 reading; excluded
  from the rule until reviewed.

**Decisions.** As above; additionally, evaluation order per the protocol: the
rule is measured first as an offline A/B in the harness (baseline vs. rule under
the closed-loop inferred key, with an annotated-key arm as a labeled oracle
diagnostic), with paired per-piece Wilcoxon on the dev rulers, before any engine
change. The production wiring question (the app always has a tonality,
defaulting to C major, so "key-conditional" needs a notion of key confidence
before it can ship) is deferred to the engine integration decision.

**Next.**

- Implement the offline A/B and run it on DCML dev and When-in-Rome dev.
- If the paired result clears the adoption bar, design the engine tie-breaker
  (tonality-gated, this family only) and revisit the four existing family rules
  it interacts with.
