# 2026-07-20: Lever 0 m7/6 rule, offline A/B

**Goal.** Measure the rule scoped by the owner decision (entry 2026-07-20-01)
with the protocol's paired statistics, offline, before any engine change.

**Setup.** Engine commit `4b709a17` (tool-side only). Fixtures and labels as in
entries -04 (DCML span view, corrected pipeline) and 2026-07-19-01
(When-in-Rome); development splits only. Three arms per event, scored on the
clean pool at root+quality: base (neutral-context ranking as-is), closed (the
flip under the key a default `HmmKeyDetector` has inferred from the event stream
so far, claim-before-event, no claim no flip; the evaluated, label-isolated
system), and oracle (the flip under the annotated key; diagnostic). The flip:
when the chosen candidate is major6/minor6 and a near-tie candidate holds the
relative seventh reading (root a minor third below; minor7 for major6,
halfDiminished7 for minor6), prefer the seventh reading iff its root sits on
degree ii (minor7 either mode; halfDiminished7 in minor) or the leading tone
(halfDiminished7, either mode). The flip is applied to the neutral ranking
within the near-tie window, mirroring how a tonality-gated tie-breaker would act
in the engine.

**What happened.**

| ruler    | n      | base   | closed | flips (helped/hurt) | oracle | claim cov. |
| -------- | ------ | ------ | ------ | ------------------- | ------ | ---------- |
| DCML dev | 54,570 | 97.54% | 98.53% | 544 (544/0)         | 98.89% | 98.3%      |
| WiR dev  | 1,594  | 97.87% | 98.93% | 17 (17/0)           | 99.69% | 93.9%      |

Paired per-piece statistics (closed minus base):

- DCML: mean delta +0.0103, CI95 [+0.0082, +0.0127], wins/losses/ties 209/0/714,
  Wilcoxon p = 4.8e-36 (923 pieces).
- When-in-Rome: mean delta +0.0048, CI95 [+0.0010, +0.0095], 6/0/50, Wilcoxon p
  = 3.6e-02 (56 pieces).

Every flip that fired was a correction; none broke a correct label, on either
ruler, under the inferred key. The closed arm recovers about 40% of the miss
population (544 of 1,344 on DCML); the oracle arm's 736 shows the ceiling for
this scope, the gap being detector abstention early in pieces and inferred-key
error.

**Plain-English reading.** The rule was allowed to act only where both musical
traditions agree on the answer, and the measurement says that restraint bought
perfect precision: several hundred chords got renamed, and every single rename
matched the analyst. The win is statistically decisive on the large ruler and
significant on the small one, with zero losing pieces anywhere. The cost of
using the app's own inferred key instead of the annotated truth is real but
modest (about a third of the ceiling), mostly the detector warming up at the
start of a piece.

**Decisions.**

- The rule as scoped meets the protocol's accuracy criterion for adoption: a
  significant paired win on both dev rulers, zero losses, and no other pool is
  touchable by construction. Proceed to the engine tie-breaker design.
- Remaining adoption gates land with the engine change: the empty-context
  invariance guard, `tool/benchmark.sh --check` plus counters, the interplay
  with the four existing family rules, and the production key-confidence
  question from entry -01 (the app's tonality defaults to C major; the rule must
  not fire on a default nobody chose).

**Next.**

- Design the engine rule: a tonality-gated tie-breaker for this family,
  positioned above the four existing family rules, firing only under the decided
  degree conditions; decide how key confidence reaches the ranking (an
  `AnalysisContext` signal vs. app-side gating).
- Re-run this A/B through the engine implementation to confirm parity with the
  offline simulation, then the benchmark gate.
