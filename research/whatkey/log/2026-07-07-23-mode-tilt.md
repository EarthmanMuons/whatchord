# 2026-07-07: The mode tilt: tonic chord quality halves parallel confusion

**Goal.** Act on the mode-disambiguation analysis (design doc, added today)
before the test-split freeze rather than after, so the result can be part of the
reported set (decision made deliberately: the test split is still untouched, so
dev-side tuning remains protocol-clean, and entry 22's "final" declaration is
superseded by this entry). The specific idea: the mode-only extraction of the
evidence model's rejected tonic bonus. When the event's chord is rooted on a
candidate tonic with a home quality (`KeySpace.tonicQualities`), tilt the
emission within that parallel pair toward the matching mode, preserving the
pair's emission sum. Because both hypotheses in the pair share the tonic pitch
class, the tilt cannot add evidence for any other tonic: the modulation-fighting
failure that rejected the tonic bonus (entry 11) and the excursion-chasing that
removed the functional blend (entry 18) are structurally impossible here.

**Implementation.** `HmmKeyDetector.modeTilt` (log-odds units), applied to the
emission distribution before the posterior update; harness flag `--mode-tilt`.
Unit tests pin the invariant (the claimed tonic never moves under the tilt on a
modulating sequence) and the mechanism (parallel-ambiguous content resolves to
the tonic chord's quality). Defaults parity verified twice: adding the parameter
at 0 reproduced the shipped Isophonics report exactly, and after adoption the
new default reproduces the explicit `--mode-tilt 2` report exactly (the entry 11
rule, both directions).

  stability cost (Isophonics spurious p90 stays 1, median 0). Mode-specific
  hysteresis remains the listed mitigation if mode wobble ever shows up in

**Plain-English reading.** We took the one rule worth saving from the machinery
the big simplification removed: when the chord being played is the home chord of
some key, its major or minor quality says which twin of that key we are in.
Applied so that it can only choose between twins and never between different
home notes, it is mathematically incapable of causing the problems that got its
parent rules deleted. The payoff: the wrong-twin mistake, the most visible error
the app can make, is cut roughly in half on every collection we have, accuracy
goes up on both kinds of answer key at once (a first for this project), and the
blues and every other regression probe stay clean. The cost is some extra
indecision on romantic piano music that genuinely flickers between twins, where
the app's pop-focused ruler shows no such cost.

**Decisions.**

- `HmmKeyDetector.defaultModeTilt = 2` shipped; the frozen configuration for the
  test-split evaluation is now: HMM, 30 s emission memory, pure profile
  correlation emissions, Albrecht-Shanahan profiles, duration weighting, margin
  floor 0.3, self-transition 0.9, temperature 0.25, mode tilt 2. This supersedes
  entry 22's freeze declaration; nothing test-side was touched in between.
- Strength selection: 2 over 3/4 (paired wash, prefer the smallest intervention
  on the plateau).
- The overlap-set spurious increase is recorded as the known trade; mode
  hysteresis is the designated mitigation and stays future work.
