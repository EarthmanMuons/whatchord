# 2026-07-07: The dwell probe: HSMM headroom measured as small

**Goal.** Decide with evidence, not priors, whether the HSMM (the design doc's
top future model direction) is worth building before the one-shot test-split
evaluation. The probe: `selfTransition` is the single-parameter shadow of what
an HSMM adds, since it sets the geometric key-dwell distribution that
explicit-duration modeling would replace. If the system is sensitive to this
dial, richer dwell modeling has something to act on; if it is a plateau, the
dwell model is not the binding constraint and an HSMM has nowhere to work.

```sh
for st in 0.7 0.8 0.95 0.98; do
  dart run tool/whatkey/harness.dart \
    --fixtures build/whatkey-fixtures/isophonics-nc-v1 \
    --split-file research/whatkey/data/splits/isophonics-nc-v1.json \
    --detector hmm --self-transition "$st" \
    --out "build/whatkey-harness/iso-st$st"
  dart run tool/whatkey/harness.dart \
    --fixtures research/whatkey/data/fixtures/when-in-rome-v1 \
    --split-file research/whatkey/data/splits/when-in-rome-v1.json \
    --detector hmm --self-transition "$st" \
    --out "build/whatkey-harness/wir-st$st"
done   # 0.9 baselines: iso-tilt-s2 / wir-tilt-s2
```

**Results** (shipped configuration otherwise; 0.9 is the shipped value):

| selfT | iso exact (cov) | iso spur p90 | wir exact (cov) | wir spur p90 |
| ----- | --------------- | ------------ | --------------- | ------------ |
| 0.7   | 0.783 (0.87)    | 1            | 0.446 (0.71)    | 1            |
| 0.8   | 0.779 (0.90)    | 1            | 0.435 (0.74)    | 1            |
| 0.9   | 0.775 (0.92)    | 1            | 0.434 (0.78)    | 1            |
| 0.95  | 0.776 (0.93)    | 1            | 0.433 (0.80)    | 1            |
| 0.98  | 0.777 (0.94)    | 1            | 0.427 (0.82)    | 2            |

Self-transition 0.7 to 0.98 spans more than an order of magnitude in implied
mean dwell (about 3 to 50 events), and every metric responds by sliding along
the coverage-accuracy curve rather than moving off it: exact varies within about
0.008 on Isophonics with coverage moving oppositely, spurious p90 is pinned at 1
nearly everywhere, modulation matching moves by a handful of counts, and
time-to-first-claim is constant. The behavior is dominated by the emission
evidence and the posterior margin floor, not by the shape of the dwell prior. An
HSMM replaces the geometric dwell distribution with a richer family; a system
this indifferent to the dwell parameter itself gives the richer family nothing
to act on.

**Plain-English reading.** Before spending days building the fancier duration
model, we checked whether the detector even cares about the knob that model
would refine. We turned the how-long-do-keys-last assumption from "a few chords"
to "fifty chords" and the detector's quality barely moved, it just traded a
little talkativeness for a little accuracy along the curve we already chart.
When a system ignores a knob that thoroughly, upgrading the knob's mechanism
cannot pay. The clever duration model stays on the future list, and now with a
measurement attached instead of a judgment call.

**Decisions.**

- No HSMM before the test split; the shipped configuration is unchanged from
  entry 23 (`selfTransition` 0.9 sits mid-plateau).
- The design doc's HSMM bullet gets a dated pointer to this probe: pursue
  explicit-duration modeling only if some future evidence shows dwell
  sensitivity this probe did not.
- This closes pre-test exploration. The trilogy of error-class attempts
  (parallel adopted, relative measured negative, fifths structurally ruled out)
  plus this probe disposition every known lever.

**Next.** The one-shot test-split evaluation across the three frozen splits.
Nothing else is planned before it.
