# 2026-07-07: The HMM is the new champion

**Goal.** Implement design section 2c and answer whether transition-structured
inference beats the hybrid, with the sequencing decision recorded first:
fine-grained point-weight tuning stays deferred until after this architectural
change, because ingredient-level optima have twice shifted when a new layer
arrived (profiles in entry 09, the chain probe in entry 11), the metric surface
is too flat at n=59 for sixteen-parameter search, and ASAP input will move the
optima again.

**Setup.** New `HmmKeyDetector` in `lib/features/key/`: 24 hidden key states;
emissions are a softmax (temperature 0.25) over the validated hybrid's per-key
scores, so everything adopted so far becomes the observation model and blend
zeroing still ablates cleanly; transitions from a signature-distance kernel
(self-transition 0.9, fifths decay 0.5 per step, relative keys at distance zero,
mode switches halved); causal forward-algorithm filtered posterior, never
Viterbi; the claim is the posterior top key at a posterior-gap margin floor, so
confidence is a true probability for the first time. One infrastructure fix
along the way: `KeySpace.canonicalTonalities` is now ordered so list position
equals `KeySpace.index` (the HMM multiplied an index-keyed emission into a
position-keyed posterior; other detectors were internally consistent either way,
and a unit test caught it immediately).

**The decisive insight: emissions must be memoryless.** The first runs fed the
HMM the hybrid's scores at the usual 30 s decay, and it sat strictly below the
champion's curve (0.50 exact at matched 0.79 coverage): the hybrid's decay
already integrates history, so the HMM was double-counting evidence, saturating
the posterior and lagging corrections. Setting the emission half-life to one
second (effectively per-event at chord-commit rates) and letting the HMM own all
temporal integration transformed it:

| config                        | coverage | exact | MIREX | modulations | spurious |
| ----------------------------- | -------- | ----- | ----- | ----------- | -------- |
| hybrid champion               | 0.79     | 0.55  | 0.65  | 133/399     | 0, 1     |
| hmm, 30 s emissions           | 0.87     | 0.49  | 0.60  | 122/399     | 0, 1     |
| hmm, 1 s emissions, floor 0.1 | 0.85     | 0.58  | 0.68  | 216/399     | 2, 7     |
| hmm, 1 s emissions, floor 0.3 | 0.76     | 0.59  | 0.70  | 207/399     | 1, 5     |

(spurious = median, p90 per piece)

**Paired vs the hybrid champion.** At the a priori defaults (floor 0.1):
coverage +0.064 (CI95 [+0.030, +0.101], **p = 0.0007**), exact +0.029 wash (p =
0.24), the entry-08 adoption shape, plus modulation matching up 62% (216 vs 133;
median lag stays low). At floor 0.3: MIREX **+0.052 (p = 0.015, 39/15/2)**,
exact +0.045 (p = 0.07), coverage -0.025 (p = 0.04). The detector's
coverage-accuracy curve sits above the champion's everywhere measured.
Self-transition and temperature barely matter (0.8-0.99 x 0.15-0.5 all within
noise); the emission memory is the load-bearing choice.

**Behavioral suite.** At floor 0.3 the suite is fully clean: every steady-key
probe exact 1.00 with zero switches (the V/V tonicization that blipped at floor
0.1 becomes an abstention), ambiguity 8/8 and 7/7, chain 0.43. Blues still
claims F, as it must: a correct filter follows on-average evidence, and our
emissions favor F on nearly every blues event; persistence absorbs momentary
contradictions, not systematic ones. Blues remains an emissions problem, not an
inference problem. Hysteresis was tried once more over the HMM and lost to
floor-raising again (0.52 exact; stale claims), consistent with entry 07.

**Adopted: `hmm` with memoryless emissions (1 s), self-transition 0.9,
temperature 0.25, margin floor 0.3.** The detector adoption rests on the a
priori config's decisive paired coverage win and the dominating curve; the
shipped floor is an operating-point choice on the pinned calibration curve
(clean suite, significant MIREX, honest costs: slight coverage loss and corpus
spurious 1/5 vs 0/2 against the old champion). Class defaults carry the config
as shared constants the harness references (the entry-11 discipline), and a
defaults-only parity run reproduces it exactly.

**Plain-English reading.**

- An HMM finally gives the detector a memory with mathematics behind it: it
  holds a belief about the key, lets each chord nudge that belief in proportion
  to how surprising it is, and prefers staying put unless the evidence keeps
  pushing. Its confidence is now a real probability (0.93 means 93%), which the
  app can show honestly.
- The one design mistake worth remembering: we first fed it evidence that
  already contained memory, so history got counted twice, like averaging
  yesterday's average into today's. Giving it raw per-chord evidence and letting
  it do all the remembering itself is what unlocked the result.
- The headline: it catches 62% more of the corpus's key changes while being
  slightly more accurate, at the cost of being a little jumpier than the old
  champion on long stable stretches. The tension between turning quickly and
  standing firm did not disappear, but the dial for it (self-transition) is now
  a principled probability instead of a forgetting hack.

**Decisions.**

- HMM adopted as champion at the config above; the hybrid remains as the
  emission model and prior champion baseline.
- Point-weight tuning formally deferred until post-HMM ablations identify
  load-bearing weights; any such tuning precedes the one-shot test-split
  evaluation.
- Blues reclassified from "needs sequence modeling" to "needs better emissions":
  the HMM eliminated the last inference-side explanation.

**Next.** ASAP performed-input fixtures, where memoryless emissions, confidence
weighting, and the HMM's noise tolerance all get their real test; then the
ablation pass and any weight tuning it motivates; then the one-shot test-split
evaluation when the detector generation stabilizes.
