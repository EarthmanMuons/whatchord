# 2026-07-08: Public terminology for annotation granularity

No detector, metric, split, or result changed.

For the paper and reader-facing documentation, the annotation-granularity
contrast is now **local-key annotations** versus **section-key annotations**.
This replaces the draft-facing terms "tonicization-scale" and "reflex-scale" in
current prose because local-key/section-key is more immediately understandable
to MIR readers and non-specialist readers.

Historical logs are intentionally unchanged. When reading older entries,
"tonicization-scale" and "reflex-scale" both refer to the local-key side of the
contrast: labels that reward immediate response to short local key assertions.
"Section-scale" refers to the section-key side: labels that name the longer
musical home and absorb brief excursions.
